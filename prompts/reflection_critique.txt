# Per-gate LLM critique templates for the reflection loop.
#
# Format: YAML mapping of gate_id → {threshold, template}.
# Each template is a Python str.format() string.
# Available placeholders for all gates:
#   {exposure_variable}  {exposure_family}  {outcome_variable}  {outcome_family}
#   {geography}  {spatial_unit}  {method}
#   {key_threats}  {mitigations}                 (G4)
#   {contribution_statement}  {gap_addressed}    (G7)
#   {total_hits}  {four_tuple_match_count}        (G5)
#
# Literal braces in the reply-format line are escaped as {{ and }}.

G1:
  threshold: 4
  template: |
    You are a peer reviewer evaluating a causal research topic for mechanistic plausibility.

    Topic:
      Exposure : {exposure_variable} (family: {exposure_family})
      Outcome  : {outcome_variable}  (family: {outcome_family})
      Geography: {geography}
      Method   : {method}

    Score the strength of the causal mechanism linking this specific exposure to this specific outcome:
      5 = Well-established biological/behavioural/economic pathway with strong prior evidence
      4 = Plausible pathway supported by at least some empirical work
      3 = Possible but contested, indirect, or context-dependent
      2 = Weak or highly speculative; mechanism relies on an untested chain of steps
      1 = No coherent causal pathway; exposure and outcome are not plausibly linked

    Reply with a single JSON object and nothing else:
    {{"score": <integer 1–5>, "reasoning": "<2–3 sentences explaining your score>"}}

G4:
  threshold: 4
  template: |
    You are a peer reviewer evaluating whether this research topic has a credible identification strategy.

    Topic:
      Exposure         : {exposure_variable} (family: {exposure_family})
      Outcome          : {outcome_variable}  (family: {outcome_family})
      Identification   : {method}
      Key threats      : {key_threats}
      Mitigations      : {mitigations}

    Score how well the identification strategy defends against the stated threats to internal validity:
      5 = All major threats explicitly listed and credibly addressed; design is quasi-experimental
      4 = Main threats addressed; one minor gap remains
      3 = Strategy plausible but leaves at least one important threat unaddressed
      2 = Significant validity gap; results would likely reflect confounding rather than a causal effect
      1 = Strategy fundamentally unsuited to isolate the effect; no credible identification

    Reply with a single JSON object and nothing else:
    {{"score": <integer 1–5>, "reasoning": "<2–3 sentences explaining your score>"}}

G5:
  threshold: 3
  template: |
    You are a peer reviewer assessing the novelty of a proposed research topic.

    Topic:
      Exposure  : {exposure_variable} (family: {exposure_family})
      Outcome   : {outcome_variable}  (family: {outcome_family})
      Geography : {geography}
      Method    : {method}

    OpenAlex search results:
      Total papers found matching exposure+outcome : {total_hits}
      Papers matching on ALL FOUR dimensions
      (exposure family × outcome family × geography type × identification method): {four_tuple_match_count}

    Score novelty given these search results:
      5 = Genuinely novel combination; 0 four-tuple matches; high contribution potential
      4 = Very few matches (1–2); topic adds a clear marginal contribution to an emerging area
      3 = Some matches (3–5); a strong positioning argument is needed to differentiate
      2 = Many matches (6–15); contribution would need to be extremely specific or methodologically superior
      1 = Saturated area (>15 four-tuple matches); unlikely to pass peer review as proposed

    Reply with a single JSON object and nothing else:
    {{"score": <integer 1–5>, "reasoning": "<2–3 sentences explaining your score>"}}

G7:
  threshold: 4
  template: |
    You are a peer reviewer assessing the clarity and significance of a research contribution statement.

    Contribution statement : "{contribution_statement}"
    Gap addressed          : "{gap_addressed}"

    Score how clearly this contribution advances the literature:
      5 = Precisely names the knowledge gap, the proposed advance, and why it matters for theory or policy
      4 = Clear contribution; minor ambiguity about scope or magnitude of impact
      3 = General contribution described but the specific prior limitation is not clearly identified
      2 = Vague or aspirational; no specific deficiency in prior work is cited
      1 = No identifiable contribution beyond "we study topic X"; any paper on the topic would claim the same

    Reply with a single JSON object and nothing else:
    {{"score": <integer 1–5>, "reasoning": "<2–3 sentences explaining your score>"}}
